Digitizing Local Kirana (Mom & Pop) Stores

About This Project

Initial Design Brief

Design a simple, accessible solution to help local kiranawalas (shopkeepers) in Indian urban markets digitize and grow their business while maintaining control.

Problem Statement

Local kirana (mom & pop) stores are struggling with competition from online platforms like Zepto, Blinkit, and Swiggy Instamart in India. Many shopkeepers are digitally underrepresented yet trusted by their community.

Opportunity

Leverage WhatsApp Communities to offer a light, easy-to-use digital layer that:

  • Increases their visibility
  • Allows effortless catalog management
  • Enables order and delivery tracking
  • Preserves customer relationships

Design Process

Empathize

  • Field visits, contextual inquiry, and one-on-one interviews.
  • 10 grocery shopkeepers (age 30–50) and 4 customers were interviewed.

Define

  • Synthesized data using affinity mapping
  • Key pain points and desires identified for both shopkeepers and customers

Ideate

  • Explored integrations with WhatsApp Communities
  • Avoided new app creation per user request

Prototype

  • Lo-fi wireframes, registration flow, catalog builder, ordering journey

Test

  • Moderated usability testing with real users
  • Iterated based on pain points and confusion in product discovery and delivery setup

User Research

Key Questions

  • How do kirana stores operate daily?
  • What digital tools are the kiranawalas familiar with?
  • How do the kiranawalas manage inventory, billing, and delivery?
  • What challenges do customers face?

User Personas

Primary: Sandesh Salve

  • 45 years, Urban grocery shop owner
  • Wants: Digitization, increased sales, customer retention
  • Needs: Easy inventory system, customer communication, trust-building tools
  • Fears: Losing customers to 10 min delivery online stores

Comparative Analysis

Explored:

  • Dunzo: Competitor in hyperlocal delivery, integrates local shops.
  • Zepto, Blinkit, Swiggy Instamart: Centralized warehouse model.

Key Research Findings

  • Most shopkeepers prefer to avoid downloading new apps
  • High value placed on customer relationships and trust
  • Need for simple, intuitive catalog creation
  • Customers trust local shops for loose grains and quality produce
  • A WhatsApp-based model was preferred due to familiarity

Design Ideas & Strategy

Design Goals

  • No new app: Use WhatsApp Community & WhatsApp Business
  • Easy onboarding for shopkeepers
  • Catalog creation via barcode scanning
  • Hyperlocal delivery customization
  • Maintain personal trust through direct chats

Flow

Grocery Shopkeeper Onboarding

  • Register store → Verify identity → Add store photo → Set delivery slots & range

Product Catalog Creation

  • Scan barcode or add manually
  • Simple product entry: Name, category, price, quantity
  • Viewable by customers via WhatsApp chat/catalog

Order & Delivery

  • Customer browses catalog via Community
  • Adds products to cart → Selects time slot → Checkout via WhatsApp Pay or COD
  • Order summary sent in chat with delivery details

Usability Testing

Key Tasks Tested

  • Shopkeeper: Register store, create catalog
  • Customer: Search local shops/products, place order

Key Observations

  • Shopkeepers found catalog creation overwhelming at first
  • Customers confused between product search vs. shop search
  • Privacy concerns over phone numbers
  • Positive reception of WhatsApp integration

Design Enhancements

Post-Testing Changes

  • Simplified product add process
  • Pre-filled fields for common items
  • Private communication channel settings
  • Hyperlocal range maxed to 10 km
  • Clear distinction between "Search Shop" and "Search Product"

Final Features

For Shopkeepers

  • WhatsApp shop setup
  • Product catalog builder via barcode or manual
  • Custom delivery time slots and delivery fee control
  • Basic Inventory management

For Customers

  • Browse catalog in chat
  • Place orders and schedule delivery
  • Pay via WhatsApp Pay or COD
  • Order summary in WhatsApp chat thread

Reflection

“Please don’t make a new app” – resounded across nearly every interview

This UX journey reaffirmed the value of meeting users where they already are. Through thoughtful research, minimal disruption to existing behavior, and a deep understanding of user constraints, we built a system that bridges tradition with technology.

Conclusion

Project Impact

  • A scalable solution with low digital resistance
  • Empowers kiranawalas to stay competitive
  • Retains the intimacy of local shopping, enabled digitally
